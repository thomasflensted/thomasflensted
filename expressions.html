AFTER EFFECTS EXPRESSIONS

What are Expressions?

Expressions are essentially snippets of JavaScript code that you can use for various purposes in After Effects. You can use it to automate workflows, you can use it take make sure elements in your comp are placed in the right places, or you can use it to drive animations from few or no keyframes. The latter makes it significantly easier to hand off projects to other people or to go back and retime your animations. Finally, you can use it to create responsive motion graphics templates that can be used in Premiere Pro without the need to open up After Effects.

Numbers

Numbers are exactly what they sound like: Just numbers. It's the most common data type in After Effects, and what most parameters accept. Position, rotation, opacity and more all take a single number as input. Numbers don't need any specific signs to work. Try adding an expression to opacity and simply type a number between 0 and 100 in the expressions field, and you will see - when you click out of the expressions field - that the opacity value updates to whatever number you typed. No matter what you type in a field that takes a number as input, it will have to eventually evalulate to a number. Type anything else and your expression will not work.

Strings

String is a fancy programmer word for text: A string is simply text. Strings can contain any and all signs on your keyboard, but they have to be enclosed in either single or double quotes. The only parameter that takes a string in After Effects is the Source Text parameter, but it also accepts numbers. Try opening up the expression field of the Source Text parameter and play around with it. Try inputting numbers and various strings and see what happens - and if you feel confident, see what happens when you start doing math with strings. Tip: If you want to know the type of something, put "typeof" before the thing you want to know the type of and finish it with ".value".
